## Email 3: The Overhyped List (Day 7)

**Subject:** 5 popular AI tools that aren't worth the hype
**Preview text:** Hot takes incoming. Don't @ me.

---

Time for some honesty.

I review a lot of AI marketing tools. Some are incredible. Some are riding the AI hype wave with a thin wrapper on GPT-4 and a $49/mo price tag.

**Here are 5 popular tools I think are overhyped:**

**1. ChatGPT (for marketing specifically)**
Incredible technology. Terrible marketing tool when used raw. Purpose-built tools like Jasper or Surfer with brand context, SEO integration, and workflows produce significantly better marketing output. ChatGPT is a great assistant — not a marketing platform.

**2. Copy.ai**
Pivoted from copywriting to "GTM AI" workflows. The workflow automation feels half-baked compared to Zapier/Make + a quality LLM. Jack of all trades, master of none. (The free tier is generous though — I'll give them that.)

**3. Triple Whale**
Very popular on DTC Twitter. But the attribution data isn't dramatically better than Meta's Conversions API + GA4 for most brands under $50K/mo ad spend. Pretty dashboard; debatable incremental insight.

**4. Brandwatch**
Enterprise social listening with enterprise pricing. For 90% of brands, Brand24 at $79/mo gives you 80% of the insights at 10% of the cost.

**5. Jasper** (partially)
Still the best enterprise AI writer. But at $49+/mo, hard to justify for solo marketers when Claude + Frase covers most needs for less. The brand voice features are the real value — if you're not using those, you're overpaying.

**The honest takes are what make this newsletter different.** I'd rather lose a potential affiliate deal than mislead you.
